Common questions

What is a Health Key?

A Health Key is a single screen that shows your identity, your provider, the service you've booked, and the maximum you'll pay. Think of it like a boarding pass for healthcare. You present it when you arrive, and both you and the provider know exactly what's authorized.

Do I need insurance to use OpenDoc?

No. OpenDoc uses clear, upfront cash prices. There's no insurance card to present, no network to worry about, and no claim to file. You see the price, you pay the price.

Can I use my HSA or FSA?

Yes. Healthcare services on OpenDoc are eligible expenses for HSA and FSA accounts. You pay with your HSA/FSA card just like any other medical payment.

What if I need something that wasn't included?

Every service on OpenDoc lists what's included and what's excluded before you book. If your provider recommends something outside the scope of your booking, your Health Key state changes to DECISION REQUIRED. Nothing additional happens without your explicit authorization and a clear price.

How much does OpenDoc cost?

Free to patients. You pay the price listed for the service you book — nothing on top. No subscription, no membership fee, no hidden platform charge.

Are the prices usually cheaper than insurance?

For many services — especially if you haven't met your deductible — the clear cash price on OpenDoc is lower than what you'd pay through insurance. You can always compare before you book.
