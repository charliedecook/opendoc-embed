How Cleared Pricing Works

Patients purchase your services directly

A Cleared price is the all-in cost you set for a specific service you offer. When a patient books through your website, they are purchasing that service from you at the price you've set. It's a straightforward transaction — just like buying any other professional service.

You set the price. The patient pays it. That's the whole transaction. The Cleared price is the guaranteed maximum the patient will pay for that service. No surprise charges, no add-on fees. If the visit requires more of your time than expected, you've already been paid. If additional services are needed — imaging, a follow-up procedure, a specialist referral — those are separate services the patient can purchase individually, whenever they're ready.

Each service you offer gets its own Cleared price. Patients can purchase as many services as they need, one at a time, each as a clear and complete transaction.

Common Questions

Questions from providers

Does this replace my EHR or scheduling system?

No. This adds a new capability to your existing website. Your EHR, your scheduling system, your clinical workflows — all unchanged. This is a new channel for patients who want to book and pay directly.

What if the patient needs additional services after the visit?

Each service is a separate purchase. If a patient comes in for an Orthopedic Consultation and you recommend imaging or an injection, those are separate services the patient can purchase individually — either in your office or through your website. They buy what they need, when they need it.

What if the visit takes longer than expected?

The Cleared price covers the service as defined — the patient has purchased that service and the price doesn't change regardless of how long it takes. You set the prices, so price them at a level that accounts for the range of what a visit might involve.

When do I get paid?

3–5 business days after you confirm the service was delivered. No claims to file. No denials to manage. No 90-day accounts receivable cycle. Payment goes directly to your bank account.

Can I change my prices?

Anytime. Update your Cleared prices in your OpenDoc dashboard and they update on your website automatically. Patients who have already purchased are locked at the price they paid.

What if a patient cancels?

Full refund within 72 hours. After that, a cancellation fee applies. No-shows receive a partial refund — you receive a portion for the reserved time. All policies are displayed to the patient before they purchase.

What about the intake questions?

In your OpenDoc dashboard, you attach custom questions to any service — medical history, allergies, current medications, prior imaging, whatever you need. Patients complete these on their phone after purchasing. When they arrive, your staff scans the Health Key and the completed intake is right there.

What does this cost me?

OpenDoc takes a small percentage of each transaction. You can build this into your Cleared price — it's your cost of acquiring a patient, collecting payment, and eliminating your billing workflow for that visit. Most providers find this is far less than what they spend on billing staff, claim denials, and collections today.

Can patients use their HSA or FSA?

Yes. HSA and FSA debit cards are accepted as payment methods. Each transaction generates a receipt that meets IRS documentation requirements for qualified medical expenses.
