Dr. Michael Torres, MD

Orthopedic Surgery — Adult Reconstruction & Joint Replacement
Total Joint Specialists · Canton, GA
Board certified — ABOS GA license active Health Key Bound
Specialization
Primary and revision total joint replacement of the hip and knee. Evaluation and surgical management of degenerative arthritis, avascular necrosis, and failed prior joint replacement. Outpatient joint replacement in an ambulatory surgery center setting.
Best fit for
  • Bone-on-bone hip or knee arthritis
  • Failed conservative treatment (PT, injections, NSAIDs)
  • Avascular necrosis of the hip
  • Failed prior hip or knee replacement
  • Candidates for outpatient joint replacement
Not the right fit for
  • Soft tissue injuries (ACL, meniscus, rotator cuff)
  • Spine or back pain
  • Foot and ankle conditions
  • Hand or wrist conditions
  • Pediatric orthopedics
  • Sports medicine without joint degeneration

What to expect
Step 1
Surgical evaluation
In-person. Review imaging, confirm candidacy, discuss implant options. ~45 min.
Step 2
Pre-op clearance
Medical clearance from PCP. Labs, EKG if indicated. 2–4 weeks before surgery.
Step 3
Surgery day
Outpatient. Home same day. General or spinal anesthesia. ~90 min OR time.
Step 4
Recovery
PT begins within days. 3 post-op visits over 90 days. Daily activity in 4–6 weeks.
Before you commit
  • Bring recent knee X-rays (weight-bearing AP + lateral). DICOM or CD accepted.

  • Medication list, especially blood thinners
  • BMI above 40 may require optimization before surgery
  • Driver required on surgery day; help at home for 3–5 days
Scope change trigger: If intraoperative findings require augments, bone grafting, or conversion to a constrained implant, the price may change. You will be notified before any change is authorized.

If This Isn't the Right Fit

OpenDoc routes to the right care — not just to a transaction.
Seek urgent evaluation
Sudden joint swelling with fever, inability to bear weight after injury, or signs of infection around a prior surgical site — go to the nearest emergency department.
You may need this first
If you have not yet had weight-bearing X-rays of the affected joint, most evaluations will require them. Your PCP can order these, or use a direct imaging center.

If you have not tried at least 6 weeks of conservative treatment (PT, anti-inflammatory medication, activity modification), Dr. Torres may recommend starting there.
